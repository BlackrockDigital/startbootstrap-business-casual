Psychological Testing

Educational and/or treatment planning for children, adolescents and adults with learning, behavioral or emotional problems is most effective when it begins with a thorough and comprehensive evaluation. Psychological testing is an important tool to use when trying to ascertain whether learning, developmental, neurological, emotional or behavioral factors contribute to a person’s problems at home, school or work.

Clients participating in the psychological evaluation program will receive:

  • • specialized assessment of Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, Pervasive Developmental Disorders, Mood Disorders, Anxiety Disorders and Learning Disabilities
  • • services administered directly by Dr. Jacobs, an experienced Clinical Psychologist
  • • diagnostic interviews and psychological testing are used to provide a thorough, individualized evaluation
  • • coordination of care via communication with schools, physicians and other service providers
  • • a comprehensive written report to be used in educational and or treatment planning
  • • recommendations utilizing strengths to address weaknesses identified in the evaluation
  • • feedback session with the client and/or parents to review the report, and to discuss the results of the evaluation
  • • in child assessments, a brief feedback session with the child is also recommended to verbally inform a child of his or her strengths and weaknesses, and to discuss recommendations as appropriate to the child’s age
  • • the brief child session is included in the feedback session fee, but is generally held at a different time than the feedback session with the parents
  • • insurance accepted for plans that Dr. Jacobs participates with, pending appropriate authorization
  • • most insurance plans do not cover educational testing, but will cover some psychological tests to assess other areas
